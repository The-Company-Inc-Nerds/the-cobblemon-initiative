# Quests: Nifl Town

A frozen town where the cold keeps its own records. Nifl's gym is a **stealth approach** before it
is a battle, and the Ice Shrine sits out on the glacier. The Company's cover-up is leaking here — the
money went quiet, and the archives remember more than the living do.

## Gym 9 — The Whiteout Gym (Ice Badge ❄️)

> **Leader Boreas** · corridor arena at `[3608 112 2031]` · **cap → 74** · **memory fragment 9**

**Entry cap: 68** (from the Dragon Badge). Boreas's ace sits at **70**.

### The gimmick — the Whiteout Approach

Three **Frost Sentinels** watch the corridor to the leader. Being **seen** debuffs you going into
the fight; crossing **unseen** earns Boreas's respect (and no debuff). This is a stealth puzzle, not
a footrace — read the sentinels' sightlines. (See [[Guidebook Gym Mechanics]] for the sight rule.)

## Side Quests

> [!NOTE]
> **Designed and in authoring.** Nifl's three town side-quests — including the run's first clean on-screen **stand-downs** (Company staff quietly refusing to fight you) — are specced but not all playable yet. All three open the moment you arrive from Frostveil Pass, before you ever challenge Boreas.

| Quest | Giver | What you do | Reward |
|-------|-------|-------------|--------|
| **Cold Storage** | Auditor Corvin | Thaw and read three frozen ledger cores; get past the archive guard | ~564 CD + 5× Rare Candy + training pack |
| **Stand Down at the Frostgate** | Warrant Officer Dain | Fight, talk down, or pay past a veteran holding the gate | up to 480 CD (fight) / 244 CD + Ice Stone (stand-down) |
| **The Long Memory** | Keeper Vetra | Relight four dark lanterns around the frozen lake | ~282 CD + a cold-town keepsake |

> **Keeper Vetra** also works as the town's rumor hub — her opening lines point you at whichever quest you haven't finished, and gossip the road ahead: Scorchspire down the Cinderfall Descent, and the crowns waiting in the capital past the last badge.

### Cold Storage

The marquee quest, and the coldest thing in a cold town. **Auditor Corvin** — a burned-out Company records clerk who stayed at his post after head office swore the vault was emptied — wants three frozen **ledger cores** thawed and read before "Retention" comes to purge the room. Core one is a liberated field still printing honest, pre-monopoly wheat prices; core two is two contradictory cover-stories in one folder; core three is a portrait the archive should not have kept. **Records Officer Halden** stands over the last drawer — and here Nifl's mercy shows: fight him (opt-in, under your cap), pay a toll to slip past, or simply meet his eyes and let him **place the face and step aside** for free. Reward: ~**564 CD** + **5× Rare Candy** + a training pack. The archives here remember a name no one in town will say aloud; Corvin never explains whose, and neither will the ice.

### Stand Down at the Frostgate

The run's first on-screen **defector**. **Warrant Officer Dain**, a twenty-year Company veteran frozen to his post at the town boundary, recognizes you mid-sentence — and his orders and his eyes stop agreeing. He hands you the choice out loud, three ways:

- **Fight** — a double battle against Dain and a Frostgate grunt (opt-in, under your cap). Prize up to **480 CD**.
- **Stand down** — he salutes the face he once saluted and walks off into the snow rather than raise a hand. **~244 CD** + an **Ice Stone** keepsake. Smaller purse, because you skip the fight — grief, not a victory.
- **Pay the toll** — buy your way quietly through the gate.

All three open the road out; Dain points you toward Scorchspire down the Cinderfall.

### The Long Memory

The battle-free one, and the quiet one. **Keeper Vetra**, the frozen-lake elder in Boreas's cold tradition, walks you lantern to lantern around the ice. Four memory-lanterns have gone dark; relight them and the lake **gives back** the small things it has kept — a wedding, a name called after a lost dog, someone very small deciding on their first snow that the world is good. The fourth lantern holds something that is *not a townsperson's at all* — a half-heard name the ice has been keeping for someone who never came to claim it. No fight, no gate beyond arrival; the vignettes are the reward, plus about **282 CD** and a cold-town keepsake.

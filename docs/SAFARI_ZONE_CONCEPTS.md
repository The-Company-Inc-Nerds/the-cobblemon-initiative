**Ruling:** the Preserve chassis ships (Concept 1 wrapper: permit, safari balls, clock,
catch-only battle-cancel, Acquisition + Custody specimens, pure sink), but the interior
loop is a LURE GAME — you don't wander to find Pokémon, you scatter bait and see what
answers. Rulings locked: **Acquisition = end-of-visit declaration with chat vote**;
**no Talent Acquisition lifeline, ever** (pure hardcore, the Preserve stays a sink).

## The lure loop

1. **Bait types** (renamed items, kiosk-sold, printed prices — all sink):
   - Honey Smear → bug / fairy / grass table
   - Dried Skewer → predator / dark / fire table
   - Glimmer Grit → rock / steel / electric table
   - Still-Water Chum → water table (pond paddock only)
   - Executive Blend → rare table (Executive Permit tier only)
2. **Scatter:** right-click ground with a bait item (UseBlockCallback — LootChestManager/
   DocPropManager precedent) → bait consumed → "The grass stirs…" actionbar → after a
   5–15s suspense window, 1–3 wild spawns roll from the bait's table at the spot
   (PokemonProperties.createEntity — the noble/ spawn pattern, verified live).
3. **The window:** lured mons are catchable ~60–90s, then wander off (tracked discard;
   letmedespawn interplay). Sprinting in early = flee; sneaking extends the window.
4. **Warmed spots:** a successful catch warms that bait spot (+1 rarity tier for the
   next scatter this visit) — keep working one spot vs spread the map. Risk/reward.
5. **Catch detection:** third POKEMON_CAPTURED subscriber (NuzlockeInit + NobleEncounterInit
   precedent), session-gated; increments ci_safari_catches, feeds the dex ladder.
6. Everything else = Preserve chassis: battle-cancel guard inside the polygon (the
   load-bearing hardcore-safety item), ball clawback at exit, specimens to the Company
   Custody box, optional post-DJ liberation flip (kept from the hybrid).

## Concept 1 — The Biodiversity Asset Preserve™ (Company Safari Zone)

The Company fenced off the region's richest wildland, rebranded it a "Biodiversity Asset Preserve," and now sells Day Permits to hunt on land it seized — Verified Access, Verified Value. The player pays the villains real CobbleDollars for twenty Safari Balls and fifteen minutes on the clock, catch-only, no battles, unspent munitions confiscated at egress. It is a pure currency sink wearing a khaki vest — and somewhere in the intake archive, the charter still carries a signature the staff were told to forget.

### Rules
- Entry by Day Permit only: 1,500 CD at the Intake Concierge — payment declined means the gate stays shut (no credit, see waiver).
- A permit issues 20 Safari Balls (Company property, non-transferable) and starts a 15:00 site clock on a countdown bossbar.
- Inside the Preserve it is CATCH-ONLY: throw balls directly at wild Pokemon (Cobblemon out-of-battle capture); battles are refused by Preserve policy — your party stays holstered, nothing can faint.
- Only issued Safari Balls may be thrown inside (stream rule; the Concierge audits your pockets on exit).
- The session ends when the clock hits 0:00 OR the last ball is spent — you are escorted (teleported) to the gate and unspent balls are confiscated.
- NUZLOCKE: the Preserve is ONE area for the entire run — exactly one safari catch ever, 'the Acquisition,' may join your battling roster; dupes clause applies.
- Every other catch is a SPECIMEN: filed permanently in the Company Custody PC box — never battles, never evolves, counts for Pokedex progress and donation milestones.
- Specimen donations pay in progress, not money: dex count feeds the 15/30 second/third starter unlocks, and one-time milestones (10 / 25 specimens) grant training packs.
- Re-entry is unlimited — every visit is a fresh permit and a fresh fee; specimen counts persist, the Acquisition slot never resets.
- The Ranger Kiosk inside sells top-ups: +5 balls for 400 CD (clock unaffected) — posted price, no haggling.
- Executive Permit (4,000 CD, unlocks at 10 donated specimens): 30 balls, 20:00, and Tier-2 paddock access with the rare spawn table.
- Hardcore safety: no battles inside means no faints, no Dark Urge, no sacrifices; hostile mobs are suppressed; timer and ball-out exits are teleports and never deal damage; logging out mid-session forfeits it.

## Concept 2 — Talent Acquisition — The Company Preserve (Safari Zone)

The Company runs the region's only game preserve as an HR department: every time the Nuzlocke takes one of yours, Talent Acquisition issues a Condolence Requisition — redeem it, plus a painful processing fee that tracks the instability index, for eight minutes, ten balls, and exactly one replacement hire. It is the run's only box-refill, and every use of it is the Founder's old logic ("assets fail, you replace them") made playable — the lifeline that keeps the run alive is owned by the villains, and it costs a little of your soul each visit.

### Rules
- The Preserve opens after badge 4 (proposed) — Talent Acquisition does not process unproven trainers; the gate dialog is score-gated on memory_fragment >= 4.
- Entry requires one Condolence Requisition — issued automatically, 1:1, whenever the Nuzlocke takes a party member; you can bank at most 3 ("condolences do not accrue").
- Entry costs a processing fee in CobbleDollars, computed from the instability index, printed on the gate button before you click, and never refunded — the Company does not refund unrealized assets.
- The gate issues 10 Safari Balls ("requisition forms"); you may bring extra Safari Balls bought at the mart, but no other ball counts inside — anything else caught in the Preserve is confiscated (auto-released) on the spot.
- Inside there are no battles: curated wilds spawn near your level cap, will not fight you, and can only be taken by thrown Safari Balls.
- A session runs 8 minutes on a visible countdown bar and ends at your FIRST successful catch, at zero balls, or at zero time — whichever comes first.
- One catch per session, maximum — the requisition is consumed on approval, not on delivery.
- The standard duplicate clause stays live inside: a duplicate catch auto-releases, even if it was your one catch — check your ledger before you file.
- When the session ends you are escorted (teleported) to the gatehouse and unused issued balls are cleared from your inventory — Company property.
- Safari catches are licensed exceptions: they never consume, and never count as, any route's first encounter — the Preserve is not a route.
- Hardcore stands: you can still die in the Preserve the ordinary ways (the terrain is flat parkland and the facility itself never deals damage), but no battle, faint, or whiteout can originate inside it.

The encounter is COMMITTED by the first Safari Ball thrown at a non-duplicate species in any licensed session: a successful capture fills the area slot; if the session ends (balls out, timer out, target lost, or confiscation) without landing the committed target, the slot burns PERMANENTLY — no re-rolls across future licenses (re-entry is for noble content and flavor only). Duplicate species are exempt targets per the mod's existing DuplicateHandling (auto-release means they cannot fill or burn the slot). Noble prizes sharing the zone (Mew's chase, Alpha Zorua) are boss-earned assets, canonically OUTSIDE the wild first-encounter rule — they never touch the safari slot. HARDCORE SAFETY: the catch-only rule is what makes this death-proof — no player Pokémon ever enters battle inside, so NuzlockeInit's faint-damage and whiteout player.kill() paths structurally cannot fire (remember: safe-zone flags do NOT suspend Nuzlocke death — ENGINE_FINDINGS is explicit). Custodians therefore punish with fees and confiscation, NEVER forced battles. If the showrunner later wants weakening battles allowed inside, that requires the stadium_active-style no-death guard in NuzlockeInit FIRST — do not ship battles-in-safari without it.

## Scores

### Concept 1 — The Biodiversity Asset Preserve™
- **Stream entertainment: 8.** The classic safari loop (countdown bossbar, last-ball clutch, out-of-battle throw aim-skill) is proven TV, and the one-Acquisition-ever declaration is a genuine chat-vote set piece. The dex-race tie-in gives it recurring-segment legs no other concept has. Loses a point only for being the least novel mechanically.
- **Engine cheapness: 8.** The only concept whose feasibility verdict is "sound." Almost everything is EXISTS: the shipped zone, the verbatim pay-probe, vanilla give/clear ball accounting, native out-of-battle capture, DexScoreManager, the dialog pipeline. Its two honest Java items are small-and-verified (BATTLE_STARTED_PRE is cancelable with a settable branded `reason`) and large-but-templated (session manager on the shrine/noble patterns). Crucially, its feasibility pass *dissolved* its own hardest fork — coordinate-boxed `spawn_pool_world` conditions mean boosted spawns are zero Java. One cited primitive was wrong (turnin is item-based) but the fix is a scoreboard latch, not a redesign.
- **Nuzlocke/hardcore safety: 8.** The only design where "no death path can fire" is structurally true as written: catch-only is hard-enforced by canceling battle creation, so faint-damage, flee-kill, sacrifice, and whiteout are unreachable inside. Residual risks are real but bounded and named: hazard-audit both teleport pads, the wander-in creeper (fence is a safety feature), the `setCaughtToZeroHP` fainted-on-arrival ruling, and the single-player logout latch.
- **Narrative fit: 9.** An amnesiac ex-CEO paying his usurped company for a permit to walk land he chartered is the thesis in one transaction, and LORE_BIBLE's scrubbing canon ("a signature on every charter") gives the intake-archive beat direct textual support. The pure-sink economy is exactly what CLAUDE.md says CobbleDollars is for.

### Concept 2 — Talent Acquisition (Condolence Requisition)
- **Stream entertainment: 8.** The condolence memo landing seconds after a death is the best single beat in any of the three concepts, and the grief-indexed gate price as a plot barometer is brilliant. But sessions end at the first catch and only unlock via deaths — screen time is irregular and each visit is short; it's a moment machine, not a segment machine.
- **Engine cheapness: 5.** Needs-rework, and the reworks are structural: the natural-Cobblemon-spawn suppression hole (Cobblemon's spawner bypasses MobSpawnMixin entirely — battleable wilds spawn inside today), a noble-scale curated spawner, the PC-side duplicate-release fix, ball provenance markers, and an unbattleable-capture canary. The biggest Java bill of the three by a clear margin.
- **Nuzlocke/hardcore safety: 5.** As written, its safety claims are falsified by its own feasibility check: the spawn hole re-arms every death path, the safari_active guard list misses the flee-with-one-mon instant `player.kill()`, and the ruling leans on a DuplicateHandling edge that is broken when the party is full and OFF by default (the documented config-leak class). All fixable — none fixed in the design.
- **Narrative fit: 10.** This is the Dark Urge thesis made playable: "assets fail, you replace them" as a service the player buys. The Company monetizing the Nuzlocke's grief is the sharpest possible expression of LORE_BIBLE §6 ("the cost of treating people as inventory"), and HR-speak-played-straight is literally line 176. The one tension — it softens permadeath in a "brutal" charter — is loss-bounded by design and is itself the moral content.

### Concept 3 — The Company Preserve (stealth heist)
- **Stream entertainment: 7.** "HE'S LOOKING" chat spam and a stealth-catch heist is the most novel pitch — on paper a 9. But the engine undercuts the mechanism: Easy NPC has no patrol objective at all, and custodians must run random-look-only (LOOK_AT_PLAYER auto-tracks and makes every catch witnessed). Witnessing therefore keys on random head turns — fines will *feel* arbitrary, which is frustration, not tension. The liberation return visit is a genuinely great payoff beat, though.
- **Engine cheapness: 5.** Needs-rework: same spawn-suppression hole, circle-vs-polygon containment misattribution, the fine/price matrix multiplying dialog entries, the DuplicateHandling config gap that its "free practice targets" rule silently depends on, plus heavy map-authoring (patrol posts, sight-cone tuning) for a mechanic the engine only half-supports.
- **Nuzlocke/hardcore safety: 4.** The headline "hardcore-safe by construction" is false as designed, by explicit choice — fine-not-block means a panicking streamer who starts a weakening battle and flees with one mon dies inside the "death-proof" zone. Its own feasibility check inverts the recommendation to cancel-Pre; the design as submitted didn't. Also note a project-invariant collision the feasibility undersold: randomness is banned on punishment surfaces (ENGINE_FINDINGS §3), and a fine triggered by a random head-turn is a rolled punishment in spirit.
- **Narrative fit: 8.** "Your Visitor License does not include acquisition rights" and "The Company doesn't kill trespassers — it invoices them" are excellent, and the post-DJ liberation flip is the only concept that gives the zone an Act-2 arc. Slightly behind the others because the heist framing makes the *player* the thief, which muddies the reclamation theme.

## Synthesis: Recommended Concept

**Ship Concept 1 as the chassis, with two grafts.** It is the only concept that is sound as written, its safety story is structural rather than aspirational, and its economy is the pure sink the plot wants. Graft:

1. **Concept 3's liberation beat.** After DJ falls, the Preserve goes free-entry and the custodial branding drops — the `field_freed` activeWhenObjective pattern generalizes, and it gives the zone a second act. Accept the known caveats: MF map subtitle changes need a relog, and the "Corporate owned" → sanctuary flip needs two overlapping zone entries with opposite holder scores.
2. **Concept 2's fee-as-barometer, in stepped form.** Keep Concept 1's printed-before-commit fees, but step them per act (Act 1 / Act 2 "price adjustment" / free post-liberation) rather than holding flat — instability is the sanctioned price driver and stepped tiers are the documented pattern (LORE_BIBLE §9), so this is canon-compliant without the per-tick @score button-mirror machinery Concept 2 needed for a continuous formula.

Do **not** graft Concept 2's replacement-hire lifeline into 1.0 — but don't discard it. It is the best narrative idea on the table and it is a *separable service* (a second desk at the same gatehouse, gated later). Surface it as a showrunner decision rather than an engineering commitment, because it changes run difficulty philosophy, not just content.

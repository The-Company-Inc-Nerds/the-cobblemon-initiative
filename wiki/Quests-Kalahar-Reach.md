## Gym 6 — The Mirage Gym (Ground Badge 🏜️)

> **Leader Gaia** · dune arena at `[2085 126 4050]` · **cap → 56** · **memory fragment 6**

**Entry cap: 50** (from the Water Badge). Gaia's ace sits at **52**.

### The gimmick — heat mirages

Six **shimmering duplicate trainers** are scattered across the dunes. Most are **mirages** that poof
when you reach them — the real ladder hides among the fakes. Chasing every shimmer is a good way to
waste a heat-stroke afternoon; learn to read which is real. Softening the true trainers still drains
Gaia.

## Side Quests

> [!NOTE]
> The two tracked side-quests (*The Reach Remembers*, *Dry Season*) are compiled and playable; the
> Dry Well rumor hub and the Grain-Buyer caravan are supporting beats around them. Everything here is

Kalahar Reach is where the wheat war stops being a rumour and becomes the ground under your boots — drained wells, a Company survey crew out at the Oasis cooking sample numbers to justify diverting the aquifer to a seized granary, and caravans that have started keeping books on your face.

### The Dry Well (rumor hub)

**Well-Keeper Marisol** (@ 2040 136 4100) stands beside a dead well in the middle of town — the local greeter, and the one who knows only that the wells died in a single night and the caravans went from rude to dangerous the same week. She points you at each open job and hands you *Dry Season* herself. No cash, no fight; she's the map. She'll also nod east toward Cyber City, "the wired town past the gap."

### The Reach Remembers

**Warden Ossa** (@ 2050 129 4085), the town elder and keeper of the old land records, holds that the northern fields the Company "sold" were common land all along — proof buried in **three basalt survey stones** the dunes swallowed a lifetime ago. Clear the sand off all three; the last, on the Old Caravan Road, is parked on by a Company Land Surveyor — **fight him** (an opt-in, above-cap wager: 400 CD win / 200 loss / 150 to decline) or read his own boundary law back at him. File the counter-claim with Ossa for **600 CD + a Hard Stone / Soft Sand / sand bundle**. It's also a quiet lore beat: the third stone carries an old personal seal Ossa half-recognises — and she talks herself out of what she's seeing.

### Dry Season

Picked up from **Well-Keeper Marisol** (@ 2040 136 4100). The wells didn't die on their own — a Company **site-survey crew** at the **Oasis** (@ ~1740 114 4191, southwest, in live wilderness) is running a **Survey Bot**, spiking the aquifer with sample readings to justify diverting it north to the granary they took, filed as "yield optimization." Trek out through spawning country, drive off the **Survey Officer** and the **Survey Foreman** (Lv 50–51), then **spike the Survey Bot's samples** so the Company's numbers come back worthless and the Oasis stops being worth their while. The Foreman is rattled by your face, not reverent, and stands down muttering that his memo said to route *around* you. Report to Marisol for **750 CD + Mystic Water ×2 + Super Potion ×2**, and the wells rise again.

### The Grain-Buyer Caravan

No quest-giver — it finds you. Once you've liberated **four or more wheat fields** across the region, the Kalahar grain caravan stops selling and turns on you: a two-buyer **doubles ambush** on the Old Caravan Road (aces at Lv 51, still under the leader's ace). It's the loudest the Company's people get before the endgame — the buyers name your face outright, as something out of a memo they were warned about. Beat them for **500 CD**. You can stand down and walk it back; the ambush only arms once the field campaign is well underway.

wheat-caravans will **ambush** you once you've freed enough fields — and the horizon plays tricks.
The Ground Shrine sits out in the sand nearby.

## Gym 6 — The Mirage Gym (Ground Badge 🏜️)

> **Leader Gaia** · dune arena at `[2085 126 4050]` · **cap → 56** · **memory fragment 6**

**Entry cap: 50** (from the Water Badge). Gaia's ace sits at **52**.

### The gimmick — heat mirages

Six **shimmering duplicate trainers** are scattered across the dunes. Most are **mirages** that poof
when you reach them — the real ladder hides among the fakes. Chasing every shimmer is a good way to
waste a heat-stroke afternoon; learn to read which is real. Softening the true trainers still drains
Gaia.

## Side Quests

> [!NOTE]
> **Designed and in authoring.** Kalahar's town side-quests are firm in plan but not all playable in the current build — a preview of what's landing. Everything here is fought under your cap; the one above-cap fight is a printed, decline-able wager.

Kalahar Reach is where the wheat war stops being a rumour and becomes the ground under your boots — drained wells, a Company pump line running out to a seized granary, and caravans that have started keeping books on your face.

| Quest | Giver | What you do | Reward |
|-------|-------|-------------|--------|
| **The Dry Well** (hub) | Well-Keeper Marisol | Town greeter — points you at every open job and gives *Dry Season* | — (hub) |
| **The Reach Remembers** | Warden Ossa | Unearth three boundary stones along the dunes; clear the guarded one | 600 CD + Hard Stone / sand |
| **Dry Season** | Well-Keeper Marisol | Raid the Company pump crew at the Oasis and shut the valve | 750 CD + Fresh Water ×8 + 2× Super Potion |

### The Dry Well (rumor hub)

**Well-Keeper Marisol** stands beside a dead well in the middle of town — the local greeter, and the one who knows only that the wells died in a single night and the caravans went from rude to dangerous the same week. She points you at each open job and hands you *Dry Season* herself. No cash, no fight; she's the map. She'll also nod east toward Cyber City, "the wired town past the gap."

### The Reach Remembers

**Warden Ossa**, the town elder and keeper of the old land records, holds that the northern fields the Company "sold" were common land all along — proof buried in **three basalt survey stones** the dunes swallowed a lifetime ago. Clear the sand off all three; the last, on the Old Caravan Road, is parked on by a Company Land Surveyor — **fight him** (an opt-in, above-cap wager: 400 CD win / 200 loss / 150 to decline) or read his own boundary law back at him. File the counter-claim with Ossa for **600 CD + a Hard Stone / sand bundle**. It's also a quiet lore beat: the third stone carries an old personal seal Ossa half-recognises — and she talks herself out of what she's seeing.

### Dry Season

The wells didn't die on their own — a Company pump crew at the **Oasis** (southwest, in live wilderness) is diverting the aquifer north to water the granary they took, filed as "yield optimization." Trek out through spawning country, beat the **Yield Officer** and the **Site Foreman** (Lv 50–51), and shut the pump; the Foreman is rattled by your face, not reverent, and stands down muttering that his memo said to route *around* you. Report to Marisol for **750 CD + Fresh Water ×8 + 2× Super Potion**, and the wells rise again.

### The Grain-Buyer Caravan

No quest-giver — it finds you. Once you've liberated **four or more wheat fields** across the region, the Kalahar grain caravan stops selling and turns on you: a two-buyer **doubles ambush** on the Old Caravan Road (aces at Lv 51, still under the leader's ace). It's the loudest the Company's people get before the endgame — the buyers name your face outright, as something out of a memo they were warned about. Beat them for **500 CD**. You can stand down and walk it back; the ambush only arms once the field campaign is well underway.

# Quests: Nifl Town

A frozen town where the cold keeps its own records. Nifl's gym is a **stealth approach** before it
is a battle, and the Ice Shrine sits out on the glacier. The Company's cover-up is leaking here — the
money went quiet, and the archives remember more than the living do.

## Gym 9 — The Whiteout Gym (Ice Badge ❄️)

> **Leader Boreas** · corridor arena at `[3608 112 2031]` · **cap → 74** · **memory fragment 9**

**Entry cap: 68** (from the Dragon Badge). Boreas's ace sits at **70**.

### The gimmick — the Whiteout Approach

Three **Frost Sentinels** watch the corridor to the leader. Being **seen** debuffs you going into
the fight; crossing **unseen** earns Boreas's respect (and no debuff). This is a stealth puzzle, not
a footrace — read the sentinels' sightlines. (See [[Guidebook Gym Mechanics]] for the sight rule.)

### Cold Storage — ✅ Done

The marquee quest, and the coldest thing in a cold town. **Auditor Corvin** (at the vault mouth, `@ 3520 68 1982`) — a burned-out Company records clerk who stayed at his post after head office swore the vault was emptied — wants three frozen **ledger cores** thawed and read before "Retention" comes to purge the room. The core walk is an escorted chain on Corvin himself (core 1 → 2 → 3); no props, no block edits. Core one is a liberated field still printing honest, pre-monopoly wheat prices; core two is two contradictory cover-stories in one folder; core three is a portrait the archive should not have kept. **Records Officer Halden** stands over the last drawer — and here Nifl's mercy shows: fight him (opt-in, under your cap), pay a toll to slip past, or simply meet his eyes and let him **place the face and step aside** for free. Reward: ~**564 CD** + **5× Rare Candy** + a training pack. The archives here remember a name no one in town will say aloud; Corvin never explains whose, and neither will the ice.

### Stand Down at the Frostgate

The run's first on-screen **defector**. **Warrant Officer Dain** (Frostgate post, `[3450 70 2030]`), a twenty-year Company veteran frozen to his post at the town boundary, recognizes you mid-sentence — and his orders and his eyes stop agreeing. He hands you the choice out loud, three ways:

- **Fight** — a double battle against Dain and a Frostgate grunt (opt-in, under your cap). Prize up to **480 CD**.
- **Stand down** — he salutes the face he once saluted and walks off into the snow rather than raise a hand. **~244 CD** + an **Ice Stone** keepsake. Smaller purse, because you skip the fight — grief, not a victory.
- **Pay the toll** — buy your way quietly through the gate.

All three open the road out; Dain points you toward Scorchspire down the Cinderfall.

### The Long Memory

The battle-free one, and the quiet one. **Keeper Vetra** (lake shore, `[3500 65 1960]`), the frozen-lake elder in Boreas's cold tradition, walks you lantern to lantern around the ice. Four memory-lanterns have gone dark; relight them and the lake **gives back** the small things it has kept — a wedding, a name called after a lost dog, someone very small deciding on their first snow that the world is good. The fourth lantern holds something that is *not a townsperson's at all* — a half-heard name the ice has been keeping for someone who never came to claim it. No fight, no gate beyond arrival; the vignettes are the reward, plus about **282 CD** and a cold-town keepsake.

## The Ice Shrine

Out on the glacier, the Ice Shrine trial is a two-NPC crossing. The **Acolyte of the Frost** waits at
the frozen trailhead (`[3668 98 1999]`) and starts the timed run; **High Priest Glacius** stands at the
far end (`[3619 65 1937]`) to take your crossing and hand over the shrine crystal. The trial reveal
auto-plays when you enter the shrine zone. Optional, off-route, hazardous — see **[[Guidebook Shrines]]**.

## Where it sits on the road

Late-game and cold: the propaganda is corrupted, the stand-downs begin (Company staff quietly
refusing to fight you). Next and last gym: **Scorchspire** (Fire, gym 10). Gimmicks:
**[[Guidebook Gym Mechanics]]**.

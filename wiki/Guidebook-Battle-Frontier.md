# Guidebook: Battle Frontier

_The post-Champion sandbox — eight halls, eight Frontier Brains, one long grind of level-90→100 mastery for the 85→100 window. And the one place on the map where **nothing you love dies.**_

> **Part of the campaign guide.** This is the *how-it-plays* detail; the plain who-and-where roster lives in **[[Quests Battle Frontier]]**. The other Company facilities (Stadium / Daycare / Safari) are in **[[Guidebook Facilities]]**. Story framing is in **[[Guidebook Overview]]** and **[[Guidebook Act III]]**.

> [!CAUTION]
> **Endgame — post-Royal-League.** The Frontier only opens to a **Champion** (`royal_league_champion`). Everything here fights at **level 90–100**; treat it as the deep end.

## Safe exhibition — the graveyard rule is off

The Frontier is the endgame **sandbox**, and it drops the permadeath entirely. It is **not a wager** and **not real-stakes** — the old "risk your real party against level 100" design is gone.

- The **whole Frontier plateau is a no-death zone.** While you stand on it, a `frontier_active` region tag suppresses **all** Nuzlocke consequences — faint-damage, party-removal, and the whiteout. It's the same suspension the **Stadium** uses (see **[[Guidebook Facilities]]**), extended across the entire frontier. A faint does nothing; a wipe ends nothing. The Brains' *"my team, not yours — nothing you love dies on this floor"* flavor is simply **true.**
- Every fight is **opt-in.** You hail a challenger or Brain, and you can always just **walk away, free.** There is **no paid-decline**, no forced fight, no shame either way. A player with a single Pokémon can wander the whole plateau and never be cornered into a battle.
- What you *spend* is prestige, not partners: **hall passes** (below) are a pure CobbleDollars sink. Losing costs you nothing but the walk back to the door.

That makes each hall infinitely **retryable** — good for a grind sandbox and a live stream, and honest about the fact that this is mastery content, not another attrition gauntlet.

### Hall passes — the ledger, not a lock

Sign the Frontier ledger at **Registrar Odette** on the concourse and your **first hall pass is free.** More cost **200 CD** each at her window. Passes are a **prestige counter and a CobbleDollars sink** — a receipt beat priced to be trivial at level 85+. They **never gate a battle**: every fight is opt-in whether you hold ten passes or none. Buy them because you like the ledger, not because you have to.

## The eight halls at a glance

Every hall is a self-contained ladder — **two challengers, then the Brain** — themed around a piece of the old Company's showpiece. The gimmicks are **flavor and identity**, not separate minigames: mechanically each is a set of clean, high-level RCT battles inside the no-death zone. What differs is the team you face and the fiction you fight it in.

| Hall | Frontier Brain | Signature team | Theme |
|------|----------------|----------------|-------|
| 🗼 **Battle Tower** | Tower Tycoon **Palmer** | Dragonite · Milotic · Rhyperior | **The Climb** — every floor a promotion |
| 🏭 **Battle Factory** | Factory Head **Noland** | Magnezone · Scizor · Metagross · Porygon-Z | **Borrowed steel** — the rental hall |
| 🏰 **Battle Castle** | Castle Lord **Percival** | Empoleon · Slowbro · Dusknoir · Steelix | **The old forms** — manners and stall |
| 🎰 **Battle Arcade** | Arcade Star **Dahlia** | Blaziken · Togekiss · Garchomp · Zoroark | **The house spins** — the entertainment wing |
| ⚓ **Battle Port** | Port Admiral **Horatio** | Pelipper · Gyarados · Kingdra · **Lugia** | **Deep water** — the daily gauntlet |

Every Brain fields a top-of-band team, and several carry a signature legendary — **Lugia** on Horatio's deck, **Regigigas** behind Brandon's ancient trio, **Darkrai** at the bottom of Selene's dark.

## 🗼 Battle Tower — "The Climb" (Palmer)

The **one honest hall.** Palmer ran it back "when the Company still built things for people to enjoy instead of things to squeeze them with," and it plays straight: clear **Climber Jasper** and **Contender Mira** — every floor a promotion — then take the top-floor seat against the Tycoon himself.

- **Palmer:** Dragonite / Milotic / Rhyperior — **no gimmick, the pure skill check.** The honest man in a building the coup left behind. Beat him and the tower's summit office is yours.

## 🏭 Battle Factory — "Borrowed Steel" (Noland)

The hall built on the **rental** conceit — Noland "drew up the rental scheme myself." Diegetically nothing here is yours and nothing of yours is at risk; that's the Factory's whole voice, and under the frontier's no-death rule it's literally true.

- Clear **Technician Rex** and **Engineer Lydia**, then Noland at his own bench.
- **Noland:** Magnezone / Scizor / Metagross / Porygon-Z — a tech/steel specialist and the engineer who kept the one lending hall he actually enjoys.

## 🏰 Battle Castle — "The Old Forms" (Percival)

The keep of **manners.** Percival holds "the last of the old forms" — a formal-duel wing with a gatekeeper and a wall to pass before the board.

- Clear **Knight Aldric** and **Guard Captain Elara** (who enforce the house forms), then sit at Percival's board.
- **Percival:** Empoleon / Slowbro / Dusknoir / Steelix — a deliberate **stall wall** that drags the fight long. Old money, older rules. He fights **doubles.**

## 🎰 Battle Arcade — "The House Spins" (Dahlia)

The Company's **entertainment wing** — "one floor built for wonder instead of the ledger." Dahlia *is* the house, and the wheel is her fiction: expect a fast, aggressive, unpredictable team rather than a literal random-rule roulette.

- Clear **Gambler Fritz** and **Player Suki**, then take the last table against the Star.
- **Dahlia:** Blaziken / Togekiss / Garchomp / Zoroark — offense and misdirection, her Zoroark keeping you unsure what's real. She fights **doubles.**

## ⚓ Battle Port — "Deep Water" (Horatio)

The frontier's **open water** and its **daily gauntlet.** Horatio is the harbormaster of the deep berth — "the daily challengers are the shallows; I am the deep water."

- Clear **Captain Stern** and **Sailor Crest** (today's rotating dockers), then reach the deep berth.
- **Horatio:** Pelipper / Gyarados / Kingdra / **Lugia** — a rain-abusing crew with a legendary flagship, fought **doubles** on the heaving deck.

## 🔺 Battle Pyramid — "The Dark Climb" (Brandon)

The last wing the Company built **for wonder instead of the ledger** — a dark descent where the fiction is *bring your own light.* Brandon "woke the three ancient Regis" and remembers the day the pyramid opened.

- Clear **Explorer Marco** (the torch-run guide) and **Archaeologist Priya** (the deeper chamber), then Brandon at the bottom.
- **Brandon:** Regirock / Registeel / Regice / **Regigigas** — the golem-king and his ancient trio, "older than the Company, older than the coup." The Regis are **his team**, not maze guardians.

## 🏪 Exchange Concourse — "The Trading Floor" (Sterling)

The frontier's **entry hub** — the concourse where the ledger, the pass window, and the founding plaque all live, presided over by Market Mogul Sterling, who "kept the Port trading long after the Company forgot this wing existed."

- Clear the two duel-for-hire regulars, **Merchant Vance** and **Trader Fiona**, then face Sterling.
- **Sterling:** Zoroark / Chansey / Alakazam / Porygon-Z — Chansey stalls to buy time, Zoroark keeps you unsure what's real. He fights at the ceiling, and this is the hall the concourse counts as its seventh clear.

> This is also where you meet the two lore NPCs: **Registrar Odette** (ledger, passes, rumors) and **Caretaker Anselm** (the plaque). See **[[Quests Battle Frontier]]** for their placement.

## 🕳️ Deep Dark Cave — "The Descent" (Selene) — the capstone

The frontier's **sealed finale.** Cave Warden Selene holds the deepest wing — "the dark down here is load-bearing; it is the whole fight." Her door does not open early.

- **The nine-way gate:** Selene is sealed until you hold **all seven other Frontier Brains** *and* **both cave challengers** — **Spelunker Dirk** and **Cave Diver Luna**. Clear those, the Deep Dark door opens, and only then will she fight.
- **Selene:** a sculk-dark **doubles** team that closes with **Darkrai** — sleep in the dark. She half-recognizes the founder ("I know the face, even when the world was told to forget it") but, like everyone here, **never speaks the name.**
- Beat her and it fires `frontier_all_cleared`: the **FRONTIER CLEARED** title and a one-time **20,000 CD** grand purse. That's the whole Frontier, done.

## The lore beat — the un-scrubbed plaque

The Frontier is the **one place the Company's scrubbing never reached.** The coup that erased the founder's name from the branch offices thought a proving ground was a toy not worth the effort — so the concourse still holds the **founder's original dedication plaque**, the only signature in the region no hand ever painted over.

**Caretaker Anselm** tends it. He shook the founder's hand the day the place opened, "would know the face anywhere," and **keeps it to himself.** You can read the plaque at his window — but by design, **neither his dialog nor the read-out ever speaks the name.** The signature is legible as set-dressing; naming it is held for the Act-3 mirror (**[[Guidebook Act III]]**). It's the frontier's one quiet grace note: the absence of scrubbing, in the one wing the Company built for people to enjoy.

## The quest line

One tracked sidebar line (press `]` to track — **[[Commands]]**):

1. **Sign the Frontier ledger** at Registrar Odette.
2. **Clear the seven halls** — a live **N/7** counter that retargets to the next uncleared Brain.
3. **Face the Warden** — the Deep Dark door opens once all seven halls (and both cave challengers) are down.
4. **Read the founding plaque** — the optional lore side-beat at Caretaker Anselm.

## Where it sits

The Frontier is the **"after."** It opens only to a Royal League Champion (**[[Quests Royal League]]**), is played in the **85→100** band as post-story decompression and mastery, and **does not advance the villain plot** — it depends on nothing downstream and gates nothing. Other endgame content in the same window: the **[[Guidebook Nobles]]** and the post-league shrines (**[[Guidebook Shrines]]**). For where it sits in the story, see **[[Guidebook Act III]]**.

# Guidebook: The Wheat War

The economic spine of Act I → II. The Company is quietly swapping the region's money out from under
it — replacing the trusted **CobbleDollar** with a **wheat** commodity it controls. Pushing that
monopoly back, field by field, is what opens the HQ raid.

> [!NOTE]
> Light plot spoilers (the villain economy). No final-boss spoilers here.

---

## The ten fields

Ten **occupied wheat fields** are scattered along the routes between towns, each held by a Company
garrison (a site manager + a yield officer, sometimes with route patrols). **Beating a field's
guards liberates it** — one-way, permanent, relog-safe.

You do **not** have to clear all ten. **Liberating any 6 of the 10 opens the HQ raid** (the seventh
badge is the other half of that gate). The remaining four are optional completionist targets.

## What a liberation does

Each freed field pulls the **instability index** back **down** (the same index gyms 1–7 push *up*).
That tug-of-war is felt everywhere the money is:

- **Relief pricing.** As you free fields, the shops step down through cheaper **relief catalogs** —
  liberating the monopoly literally lowers your prices. (The index also drives a baseline price
  drift: buying early, or after liberations, is cheaper.)
- **The HUD.** The main-quest side line counts your progress **n / 6** while the campaign is live.
- **A safer route.** A liberated field stops being hostile ground.

## The wheat-traders turn on you

The Company's roving **wheat-traders** track your campaign and escalate:

| Fields freed | Trader mood |
|:---:|-----|
| 0–1 | **Polite** — they pitch you the "alternative" currency. |
| 2+ | **Suspicious** — they've noticed you, and the recognition beats sharpen. |
| 4+ | **Hostile** — they **ambush**. Standing in the way of the monopoly has a cost. |

## The gate

Hold **6 freed fields** (and 7 badges) and **Company HQ** becomes assailable — the Act II climax.
The raid, its geometry, and the "CURRENCY STABILIZED" payoff are on **[[Quests Company HQ]]**;
the narrative is on **[[Guidebook Act II]]**.
